Three things destroying your sleep.

Before any protocol works, these need to go. Each one compounds the other two.

01

Your phone in bed.

Blue light suppresses melatonin by up to 50% in the 90 minutes before sleep. Your brain thinks it is still daytime. Sleep onset delays. Quality drops.

Fix: no screens 60 minutes before bed. Start with 20 minutes if that feels impossible.
02

Your 3pm coffee.

Caffeine has a 5–7 hour half-life. Half of that afternoon espresso is still blocking your adenosine receptors at 10pm. You are not tired because of the baby. You are tired because of the latte.

Fix: hard caffeine cutoff at 1pm. No exceptions.
03

Sleeping in on weekends.

Your circadian rhythm is set by your wake time, not your bedtime. Every 90-minute lie-in is jet lag. Chronic misalignment degrades sleep quality even when total hours are fine.

Fix: same wake time every day. Within 30 minutes. Even after bad nights.

The 3-2-1 rule. Start tonight.

Three numbers. One wind-down sequence. Your nervous system learns to expect sleep.

3hbefore bed

No food or alcohol.

Digestion raises core temperature. Sleep onset requires a 1–2°C drop. Eating late delays that drop. Alcohol suppresses REM and causes early waking.

2hbefore bed

No more screens.

Phone, laptop, TV — all off. Switch overhead lights to lamps. LED ceiling lights emit enough blue light to delay melatonin. The bedroom becomes a no-screen zone.

1hbefore bed

Begin your wind-down.

Same activities, same order, every night. A warm shower, a physical book, a brief journal entry. What it is matters less than doing the same thing consistently.
