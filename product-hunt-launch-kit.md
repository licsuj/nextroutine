## 5. COMMUNITY POSTS

### Reddit — r/NewDads

**Title:** The 15-minute morning routine that actually survived my first 8 weeks as a dad

**Body:**
First-time dad here — son is 4 months old now.

Before he arrived I had a decent morning routine. Gym, journal, coffee, read. About 90 minutes. It lasted exactly 2 days after we came home from the hospital.

I spent weeks trying to get it back. It didn't work. 90 minutes of uninterrupted time doesn't exist when you're up at 4am and running on sleep fragments.

So I stripped it down to 15 minutes — and built in two fallback versions for the really bad mornings (5 min and 2 min). The key insight was this: you don't need a long routine. You need a consistent one that survives your worst day.

Here's what I landed on:

**Minutes 0–2:** Water + 60 seconds of movement (literally just stretching — nothing Instagram-worthy).

**Minutes 2–5:** One sentence in a notebook answering "what matters today?"

**Minutes 5–10:** The one thing — whatever is most important in my life right now that isn't urgent. Some days it's reading. Some days it's stretching. Today it was writing this post.

**Minutes 10–15:** Prepare for the transition. Check monitor, prep bottle, lay out what I need. This bridge between "my time" and "dad time" was the piece I was missing.

On a terrible morning: water + one sentence = 2 minutes. Done. I haven't missed a day in 11 weeks using this system.

I ended up building a whole thing around this — a site called NextRoutine with protocols for mornings, sleep, and screen time, all designed for new dads. Free newsletter if you're interested, but honestly just try the protocol above. It works.

Would love to know what's worked for other dads here. What does your morning look like right now?

### Reddit — r/productivity

**Title:** I rebuilt my entire productivity system after becoming a dad — here's the one insight that changed everything

**Body:**
Every productivity system I've ever used — GTD, time blocking, Atomic Habits, the 5am club — assumes three things: consistent sleep, predictable mornings, and a stable schedule.

When I became a dad 4 months ago, I had none of those. My sleep was fragmented, my mornings were unpredictable, and my schedule changed daily. Every productivity framework broke within a week.

The insight that fixed it: **design for the worst day, not the best.**

Most systems are designed for your best-case scenario. 60 uninterrupted minutes. 8 hours of sleep. Full cognitive bandwidth. When reality is worse than that (and for new parents it always is), the system fails and you feel like you failed.

So I inverted it. I built every routine around the worst morning I'd have that week — 4 hours of sleep, baby screaming, brain fog, zero motivation.

The result was a 15-minute morning protocol with two fallback versions:
- Full: 15 minutes
- Survival: 5 minutes
- Minimum viable: 2 minutes (water + one sentence in a notebook)

The 2-minute version means I never skip. In 11 weeks I haven't missed a single day. Not because I'm disciplined — because the floor is so low that skipping feels harder than doing it.

The neuroscience behind it: there's a 23-minute window after waking called the cortisol awakening response (CAR) where your brain's activation system is at peak. Using those minutes for your own agenda instead of your phone is the single highest-leverage productivity hack I've found as a parent.

I ended up building a site around this (NextRoutine — free weekly newsletter for dads) but the principle applies to anyone whose schedule is unpredictable: design your system for the floor, not the ceiling.

Anyone else redesigned their productivity system after a major life change?
