Privacy Policy

This policy explains what personal data NextRoutine collects, why, and what your rights are. It is written to be readable rather than impressive.

Last updated: 2 May 2026

1. Who we are

NextRoutine is a small, independent newsletter and blog for new and expectant fathers. It is operated by Justin, an individual based in the European Union, working on this in public as a side project.

For all matters relating to your personal data, you can contact us at hello@nextroutine.com. We aim to reply within 30 days, which is also the standard response time required by the GDPR.

Under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the operator described above is the data controller for the personal data described in this policy.

2. What personal data we collect

We try to collect as little as possible. The categories of personal data we collect are:

Email address (only if you choose to subscribe)

If you submit your email through one of our subscription forms, we collect your email address to send you the newsletter you signed up for. We may also collect a "stage" indicator (for example, "expecting" or "first weeks") if you provide it, so we can send stage-relevant content.

Technical data when you visit the site

When you visit any page on nextroutine.com, our hosting provider automatically logs basic technical information for security, abuse prevention, and operational monitoring. This includes your IP address, browser type, device type, the pages you visit, and the date and time of your visit. This is standard for any website.

Cookies and similar technologies

We use a cookie consent banner. Essential cookies that are needed for the site to function are set automatically. Non-essential cookies (for example, analytics) are only set if you give consent through the banner. You can change your preferences at any time using the "Cookie Settings" link in the footer.

3. Why we process your data (lawful basis)

The GDPR requires us to identify a lawful basis for each type of processing. Ours are:

  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): for sending you the newsletter and for any non-essential cookies. You can withdraw consent at any time.
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)): for the technical logs that allow us to keep the site running, secure, and free from abuse. We have weighed our interest against your privacy rights and consider the impact minimal.

4. Who we share data with

We do not sell your data. We share it only with the third-party services we genuinely need to operate the newsletter and the website. Each of these is a separate "data processor" under the GDPR and operates under its own privacy terms.

Beehiiv (newsletter delivery)

If you subscribe to the newsletter, your email address and stage indicator are stored in Beehiiv (a US-based newsletter platform) so we can send you emails. Beehiiv processes your data on our behalf as a data processor. Their privacy practices are documented at beehiiv.com/privacy.

Vercel (website hosting)

The website is hosted by Vercel (a US-based hosting platform). Vercel automatically processes basic technical information (IP address, request data) so the website can be served to you. Their privacy practices are documented at vercel.com/legal/privacy-policy.

International transfers

Beehiiv and Vercel are based in the United States, which means your data may be transferred outside the European Economic Area when you use this site. Both providers participate in the EU-US Data Privacy Framework and use Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission as additional safeguards. We rely on these mechanisms for the international transfer of your data under Articles 45 and 46 of the GDPR.

5. How long we keep your data

  • Newsletter subscribers: we keep your email address for as long as you remain subscribed. If you unsubscribe, we keep your email on a suppression list (so we know not to contact you again) but stop using it for any other purpose.
  • Technical logs: retained by our hosting provider for the period described in their own retention policy, typically a few weeks for routine logs and longer for security-relevant events.
  • Email correspondence with us: we keep emails sent to hello@nextroutine.com for as long as needed to respond to and resolve the matter, then delete or archive them.

6. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have several rights regarding your personal data. You can exercise any of these by emailing hello@nextroutine.com. Specifically, you have the right to:

  • Access — ask for a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification — ask us to correct data that is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Erasure — ask us to delete your data (sometimes called the "right to be forgotten").
  • Restriction — ask us to pause processing of your data while a question is resolved.
  • Portability — ask for a copy of your data in a portable format.
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent — at any time, where we are processing on the basis of consent. Every newsletter email contains a one-click unsubscribe link, which is the simplest way to withdraw consent for newsletter emails.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the data protection supervisory authority of the EU member state where you live, work, or where you believe a violation has occurred. A list of EU supervisory authorities is available at edpb.europa.eu.

7. Children

NextRoutine is intended for adults — specifically, expectant and new fathers. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 16. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it.

8. Security

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including HTTPS for all site traffic, restricted access to subscriber data within our newsletter platform, and secure storage of API credentials. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to reduce the realistic risks for an operation of this size.

9. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy in a way that meaningfully affects how we use your data, we will update the "last updated" date at the top and, where the change is significant, notify newsletter subscribers by email. Older versions are not maintained, but you can always contact us to ask what an earlier version said.

10. Contact

For any privacy-related question or request, email hello@nextroutine.com. A real person reads these. We will respond within 30 days, which is the maximum response time required by the GDPR for data subject requests.
