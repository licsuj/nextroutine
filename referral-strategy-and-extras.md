## PART 1: REFERRAL STRATEGY

### Recommended Reward Structure

**Winner: Unlock Pro features (tiered)**

| Referrals | Reward |
|---|---|
| 1 | 1 week Pro free |
| 3 | 1 month Pro free |
| 5 | Pro free forever |

**Why this wins for a fatherhood newsletter:**

The audience is cost-conscious new dads — they are already spending on baby gear, nappies, and everything else. Cash discounts (€2 off) feel too small to motivate action. Exclusive content unlocks create a "walled garden" problem where your best free content is hidden behind a referral wall, which reduces the shareability that drives organic growth.

The Pro feature unlock works because: (a) it gives the referrer something they already want, (b) it creates a natural upgrade path — once they've tasted Pro for a week, most will stay, (c) "Pro free forever" at 5 referrals is achievable but aspirational, and most importantly, (d) the cost to you is near-zero since the marginal cost of a digital subscription is nil, but the perceived value is €95.88/year.

The "5 referrals = Pro forever" tier is critical. It creates a small group of superfans who have earned their way in and will be your most loyal advocates. These are the dads who post about you on Reddit unprompted.

## LEAD MAGNET IDEAS (Ranked by Conversion Potential)

### #1 — Fatherhood Track: "The Pre-Birth Routine Audit" (PDF)
**Niche:** Fatherhood
**What it is:** A 5-page self-assessment that asks expectant fathers to audit their current sleep, morning, screen time, relationship, and financial habits — then scores each area and shows where they'll break first when the baby arrives.
**Why it converts:** It creates a personalised gap analysis. Once a father sees his scores, the natural next step is "how do I fix this?" — which is exactly what Pro covers.
**Conversion potential:** ★★★★★

### #2 — Sleep Track: "The 90-Minute Sleep Cycle Calculator" (Interactive Page)
**Niche:** Sleep
**What it is:** Enter what time you need to wake up → get your ideal bedtime (counting backwards in 90-minute cycles). Includes a short explanation of why this works.
**Why it converts:** Tool-based lead magnets have the highest conversion rates because they solve a specific problem immediately. The calculator creates a "this works" moment that makes the full Sleep Track (Pro) feel like the obvious next step.
**Conversion potential:** ★★★★★

### #3 — Morning Routine Track: "The No-Phone First Hour Protocol" (PDF)
**Niche:** Morning Routine
**What it is:** A 3-page protocol with the science (CAR), the rules (phone in another room for 60 min), the replacement activities, and a 7-day tracking sheet.
**Why it converts:** It's specific, immediately actionable, and produces a noticeable result within 3 days. The tracking sheet creates a habit loop that leads to "what else can I build?" → Pro.
**Conversion potential:** ★★★★☆

### #4 — Screen Time Track: "The 7-Day Phone Reset" (Email Sequence)
**Niche:** Screen Time
**What it is:** A 7-email drip (one per day) that walks the subscriber through a progressive phone reset: Day 1 audit → Day 2 phone out of bedroom → Day 3 notification audit → Day 4 grayscale → Day 5 app removal → Day 6 replacement activities → Day 7 new defaults.
**Why it converts:** Email sequences have higher engagement than PDFs because they arrive daily and create accountability. The progressive structure builds investment. By Day 7, the subscriber has already changed their behaviour — Pro becomes the tool to maintain it.
**Conversion potential:** ★★★★☆

### #5 — Midlife Reset Track: "The 40-Year Audit" (PDF)
**Niche:** Midlife Reset (pre-launch lead magnet)
**What it is:** A self-assessment for men over 40 covering health, relationships, career satisfaction, daily routine, and purpose. Scored with a "readiness for change" rating.
**Why it converts:** It captures the audience for the Midlife Reset track before it launches, building a waitlist. The audit format (like the Pre-Birth Audit) creates a gap that only the track content fills.
**Conversion potential:** ★★★☆☆ (lower because the track doesn't exist yet — but excellent for waitlist building)
