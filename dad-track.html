FAQ

The moment you find out. Pre-birth is the most underutilised window in a father's preparation. The habits you build during pregnancy — sleep timing, morning anchors, screen use — are the exact habits that survive or fail in the first eight weeks. Our Stage 1 protocols are built specifically for expectant fathers. Starting before the birth means arriving with systems already embedded, not starting from zero in the chaos of week one.
In survival mode (Stage 2), it is 10 minutes: one minute of intention setting, five minutes of minimal movement, and four minutes reviewing one priority. No gym, no journalling, no pressure. As sleep stabilises (Stage 3), the 30-minute version adds a short walk and a brief journal prompt. In Stage 4, the full 60-minute protocol becomes available. Every version is built to run on less sleep than you think you need to function on.
Both. The fatherhood track covers four stages: Stage 1 (months 1–9, pre-birth), Stage 2 (weeks 1–8, newborn), Stage 3 (months 3–12, rebuild), and Stage 4 (year one plus, identity). Every protocol here is designed to hold under those conditions — not under ideal ones. It is a stability system first, a performance system second.
