The full journey

Every new dad stage. One system.

The fatherhood routine isn't a single plan. It's a system that evolves with you — from the moment you find out to the life you're rebuilding in year one and beyond.

01
Months 1–9

Build before the storm

The nine months before your baby arrives are the most underused window in fatherhood. While everyone focuses on the nursery, you have a rare opportunity: time to build the version of yourself your child will know. Most new dads spend this time waiting and worrying. The ones who don't emerge from the newborn stage faster, sleep better during it, and are less likely to experience the identity crisis that hits around week four.

What you build at this stage:
  • Pre-birth habit audit — identify which of your current habits won't survive week one
  • Sleep banking protocol — the evidence-based approach to storing sleep before the disruption
  • Partner routine alignment — building a shared system before you're both exhausted
  • Financial routine reset — four habits that remove money stress from the first year
  • Identity audit — who you are now, who you want to be as a father, and the gap between them

02
Weeks 1–8

Survive and stay present

The newborn stage is not designed for human flourishing. You will be sleep-deprived in a way that feels cognitively similar to moderate intoxication. The research on new father wellbeing is stark: 1 in 10 new dads experience postnatal depression, and the majority of paternal identity crises occur in the first eight weeks. The 15-minute new dad morning protocol was built for this specific reality — not the 5am cold-plunge version of fatherhood, but the version where you function and stay present on broken sleep.

What the survival protocol includes:
  • The 15-minute morning protocol — designed for 4-hour sleep cycles
  • Emotional check-in system — 2-minute daily framework to catch deterioration early
  • Sleep debt management — when to nap, when to push through, and why it matters
  • Paternity leave structure — how to make the most of every day regardless of length
  • Return-to-work transition plan — the week before and the week after
✦ Stage 2 templates + survival guide in Pro
03
Months 3–12

Rebuild what matters

By month three, the survival phase starts to ease. Sleep improves enough that you can think again. This is the most important moment in the first year to act — because the patterns you establish now will calcify into habits by month six. Most dads waste this window by defaulting back to pre-baby routines that no longer fit their life. The rebuild phase is about designing a new morning, a new relationship with time, and a new physical baseline that reflects who you actually are now, not who you were before.

What the rebuild phase covers:
  • Dad morning routine rebuild — 30-minute protocol for months 3 onwards
  • Career and ambition rebalancing — how to stay hungry without neglecting presence
  • Relationship maintenance routine — what to do with the 15 minutes you have, not the evening you don't
  • Physical reset — starting movement again without guilt or obsession
  • Screen time audit — reclaiming the phone time that crept in during survival mode
✦ Stage 3 rebuild templates in Pro
04
Year 1+

Become the model

By year one, you are no longer a new dad — you are just a dad. The challenge shifts from surviving to modelling. Your child is watching you from the moment they can track movement. The habits you build now are not just for you: they are the standard your child will internalise as normal. Fathers who are consistent with their morning routine, their sleep habits, and their relationship with screens raise children who are statistically more likely to have those same habits. Stage four is about building something worth watching.

What year one and beyond includes:
  • The model routine — a full-year morning protocol designed to be visible to your child
  • Long-game ambition system — projects, goals, and growth that survive fatherhood intact
  • Partner relationship audit — annual check-in framework for the relationship underneath the parenting
  • Physical discipline — building consistent exercise without it becoming another thing you feel guilty about
  • Legacy habit audit — what are you modelling, and is it what you want?
