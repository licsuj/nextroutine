### PRO NEWSLETTER — WEEK 2

**Subject:** The 90-minute rule that explains why 8 hours still leaves you exhausted

**Stage header:**
> Your stage: [STAGE_NAME] — Week [X]
> Next milestone: [NEXT_MILESTONE]

---

You slept 7.5 hours last night. You woke up groggy, heavy, foggy. You assumed you need more sleep.

You don't need more sleep. You need better-timed sleep.

**The mechanism:**

Sleep runs in cycles of approximately 90 minutes. Each cycle moves through light sleep (N1), deeper sleep (N2), deep sleep (N3), and REM. If your alarm cuts a cycle in half — waking you during N3 deep sleep — you experience "sleep inertia": the groggy, disoriented, worse-than-before-I-slept feeling.

The fix is not sleeping more. It is timing your wake-up to land at the end of a complete cycle.

**The calculation:**

Count backwards from your wake-up time in 90-minute blocks. If you need to wake at 6:30am:

- 6:30am → 5:00am (1 cycle — emergency nap only)
- 6:30am → 3:30am (2 cycles — bad night)
- 6:30am → midnight (4.5 cycles — good night)
- 6:30am → 11:00pm (5 cycles — full recovery)
- 6:30am → 9:30pm (6 cycles — ideal)

Add 15 minutes to fall asleep. If you want 5 full cycles: be in bed at 10:45pm, asleep by 11:00pm, alarm at 6:30am.

**For new fathers:** You won't get 5 cycles for a while. But you can align what you do get. If the baby wakes you at 3:30am and you need to be up at 6:30am — that's 2 complete cycles. Set your alarm for 6:30am (not 6:00 or 6:15) and you'll wake at the natural transition point instead of mid-cycle.

### ELI5 #2 — CAR SEATS

**Subject:** Car seats — the 3 things that actually matter (and the 7 that don't)

**For Pro subscribers — full version:**

Car seat shopping is deliberately overwhelming. Brands use safety anxiety to upsell features you don't need. Here's what actually matters.

**What matters:**

1. **Safety rating.** Check your country's independent testing body (Euro NCAP in EU, NHTSA in US). A seat rated "Good" or "Best in Class" by an independent body is safe. Period. The brand's own safety claims are marketing.

2. **Ease of installation.** A car seat that's difficult to install is a car seat that will be installed incorrectly. ISOFIX (or LATCH in the US) makes installation nearly foolproof — the seat clicks into fixed anchor points in your car. If your car has ISOFIX, use it. Every time.

3. **Your baby's size, not age.** Car seats are rated by weight and height, not age. A "0–13kg" seat doesn't mean "birth to 12 months" — it means until your specific baby reaches 13kg. Some babies outgrow their first seat at 8 months. Others at 14 months. Check the limits.

**What doesn't matter:**

Fabric colour, cup holders, "travel system compatibility" (usually locks you into one brand ecosystem), built-in sun shades (fall apart), "anti-rebound bars" on Group 0+ seats (useful but not essential), premium padding (the baby can't tell), and anything described as "luxury" or "premium edition."

**The recommendation:** Cybex Aton 2 (i-Size). Consistently top-rated by ADAC and Euro NCAP. ISOFIX base available separately. Fits most travel systems. Not the cheapest — but the one that scores highest on the tests that matter. [Check current price →](#AFFILIATE_CYBEX)

**The budget alternative:** Joie i-Snug 2. i-Size compliant, excellent safety ratings, ISOFIX base available, significantly cheaper. If budget is a factor, this does everything you need. [Check price →](#AFFILIATE_JOIE)
