The problem

Three problems. One root cause.

Bad sleep, a missing morning routine, and too much screen time aren't separate issues. They're a single loop feeding itself — and breaking it starts the night before.

😴

You wake up exhausted no matter how long you sleep

Scrolling in bed spikes your cortisol and delays melatonin. Your brain never fully winds down. So you never fully wake up — and you spend the morning trying to catch a train that already left.

📱

Your first hour belongs to an algorithm, not you

Before your feet hit the floor, you've handed your attention to a feed designed to keep it. The rest of your day runs in reactive mode — responding, not doing. That's not a willpower problem. It's a structural one.

🌀

Your morning routine exists only in theory

You've bookmarked the 5am protocols, bought the journal, set the alarm. None of it sticks because there's no system that connects sleep, phone habits, and your morning into one coherent thing.

The solution

Fix the loop.
Own the day.

Bad sleep causes bad mornings. Bad mornings cause more scrolling. More scrolling causes worse sleep. NextRoutine breaks this loop by fixing all three — not as separate habits, but as one connected system that starts the night before.

1

A wind-down routine that fixes your sleep

Screen time at night isn't just a bad habit — it's the reason you can't wake up. We give you a structured evening routine that cuts phone use, protects your melatonin, and means you actually feel rested when the alarm goes off.

2

A morning that starts before you touch your phone

Not a 4am ice bath. A realistic, progressive routine built around your schedule — that protects your first hour from the feed and uses your cortisol peak for something that actually matters.

3

Screen time drops as a side effect, not a goal

When you have somewhere better to be in the morning, and something worth sleeping for at night, the scrolling just stops. No blockers. No willpower. Just a better alternative.
