New dad questions answered.

Start with the pre-birth routine audit — a structured review of your current habits that identifies which ones will survive the transition and which will collapse under sleep deprivation. Do this 6–8 weeks before your due date. It's included free in the Fatherhood Starter Pack.

15 minutes in three blocks: 5 minutes with no phone, 7 minutes of movement, 3 minutes of intention setting. Specifically designed to survive broken sleep in the first 8 weeks and scale upward as the baby's sleep improves. The full protocol is in the free Starter Pack.

Focus on sleep quality, not quantity. You can't control when the baby wakes, but you can protect the quality of sleep you do get. Fix your pre-sleep routine using the wind-down protocol, and bank sleep in the final weeks before birth. The 15-minute morning protocol is designed to function on 4 hours of broken sleep.
