Your brain has a 23-minute window.

In the first 20–30 minutes after waking, your body produces a cortisol spike called the cortisol awakening response. It drives your alertness, focus, and emotional control for the rest of the day. What you do in this window — not what you do at the gym, not what you eat — sets your neurological baseline until evening.

📱

Checking your phone in this window hijacks the entire response. You hand the most powerful 23 minutes of your day to someone else's agenda. The morning feels off before it starts — because neurologically, it is.
