I needed this to exist. So I'm building it.

NextRoutine is the fatherhood routine system I went looking for and couldn't find. I'm building it as I go through it myself — research-based, tested in real time, openly updated when something doesn't work.

What this site actually is

I am not a parenting expert. I have no clinical credentials. I have not raised three kids and figured it all out. I am one first-time father in Malta, with a software engineering background, researching this transition seriously, synthesising what works from peer-reviewed sources, testing protocols on myself in real time, and writing about what I'm finding.

If you want a guru telling you the answer, this is not the site for you. If you want someone working through this honestly — research-grounded, publicly tested, openly updated when something doesn't work — you're in the right place.

How the protocols are built

Stage 1 — Research synthesis. I read the relevant academic literature. Sleep research from peer-reviewed journals, behavioural science papers on habit formation, the actual studies behind the popular advice. Sources are cited where claims are made.

Stage 2 — Personal testing. I test protocols on myself before publishing the full Pro version. The Free tier gets the foundation. The Pro tier gets the full protocol after I've actually run it.

Stage 3 — Iteration. When a reader emails saying something didn't work for them, I re-examine and update. The site is versioned. You can see what changed and when on the changelog.

Why fatherhood specifically

Two reasons. The honest one and the structural one.

The honest one: I'm becoming a father myself for the first time. The motivation to build this was selfish first — I needed it to exist. Building it for others came second.

The structural one: Every routine resource on the internet is built for someone with stable sleep, predictable mornings, and full agency over their schedule. New fathers have none of those. The advice fails on impact with the actual conditions of fatherhood. Someone needed to build the version that survives a three-hour night and a 4am feed. So I am.

Who this is for

Expectant fathers who have nine months to prepare and don't want to waste them. New dads in the first eight weeks who need protocols that survive on no sleep. Fathers in the rebuild phase (months 3–12) who are ready to reclaim their mornings, sleep, and identity. And any father who knows that wanting to be a good dad is not enough — they need a system that holds when willpower does not.

What makes this different

Mechanism first

Every protocol explains why something works before telling you to do it. If you understand the cortisol awakening response, you will never check your phone first thing again.

Built for the worst day

Every protocol has a full version, a 5-minute fallback, and a 2-minute minimum. Because the morning your baby was up three times is the morning your routine matters most.

Honest about the source

When I write "research suggests," there's a citation. When I write "in my own testing," I mean I personally tried it. I never claim community size or experience I don't have.

What you get from being on the list

A weekly email every Tuesday. One protocol, one research note, or one update on what I tried this week. No motivational filler. No "10 things every dad should know." Just useful work, grounded in research and tested where possible.

If you go Pro, you get the full protocols, every template, and 24-hour early access to the newsletter. The Pro version exists because building this properly has real costs — research time, infrastructure, the work of testing and refining. €7.99 a month covers it.

Software engineer, based in Malta, becoming a father for the first time. I read every reply to the newsletter myself. The email is hello@nextroutine.com.
