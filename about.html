I'm building the routine system I needed.

My partner is pregnant with our first child. NextRoutine is what I went looking for and couldn't find — a fatherhood routine system grounded in research, tested in real time, written by someone going through it.

Why this exists

The internet has thousands of morning routine guides, sleep optimisation articles, and habit-building frameworks. None of them are written for a father running on four hours of sleep with a newborn who has no respect for circadian rhythms.

I went looking for the version that should exist. It didn't. So I'm building it.

Who this is for

NextRoutine is for expectant and new fathers who want a system — not just motivation — for the biggest transition of their adult life. Expectant dads with nine months to prepare. New dads in the first eight weeks who need protocols that survive on no sleep. Fathers in the rebuild phase who are ready to reclaim their mornings, sleep, and identity.

If you want a guru telling you the answer, this isn't the site for you. If you want someone working through this honestly — research-grounded, openly tested, updated when something doesn't work — you're in the right place.

What makes this different

Mechanism first

Every protocol explains why it works before telling you to do it. Once you understand the cortisol awakening response, you'll never check your phone first thing again.

Built for the worst day

Every protocol has a full version, a 5-minute fallback, and a 2-minute minimum. Because the morning your baby was up three times is the morning your routine matters most.

Honest about the source

Where I cite research, the source is linked. Where I share what I've tested, I say so. I don't claim community size or experience I don't have.

The mission

There are thousands of resources for mothers — and they deserve every one of them. But fathers get a pamphlet and a pat on the back. NextRoutine is here to close that gap.

The goal is to make fatherhood the most prepared-for transition in a man's life, not the least.
