Nobody builds routine systems for fathers. So we did.

NextRoutine exists because the self-improvement world forgot about the people who need it most — new and expectant dads navigating the biggest identity shift of their lives.

Why this exists

When my first child arrived, every morning routine I had spent three years building collapsed within 72 hours. The 5am wake-ups, the journaling, the gym — all of it gone. Replaced by fragmented sleep, 4am feeds, and the quiet realisation that nobody had prepared me for any of this.

I went looking for help. The internet had thousands of morning routine guides, sleep optimisation articles, and habit-building frameworks. None of them were built for a father running on four hours of sleep with a newborn who has no respect for circadian rhythms.

So I built one. Then I built three more. Then I realised this was bigger than my own mornings — there is an entire gap in the market where fathers should be.

Who this is for

NextRoutine is for expectant and new fathers who want a system — not just motivation — for the biggest transition of their adult life. That means:

Expectant fathers who have 9 months to prepare and do not want to waste them. New dads in the first 8 weeks who need protocols that survive on no sleep. Fathers in the rebuild phase (months 3–12) who are ready to reclaim their mornings, sleep, and identity. And any father who knows that wanting to be a good dad is not enough — they need a system that holds when willpower does not.

What makes this different

Mechanism first

We explain why something works before telling you to do it. If you understand the cortisol awakening response, you will never check your phone first thing again.

Built for the worst day

Every protocol has a full version, a 5-minute fallback, and a 2-minute minimum viable version. Because the morning your baby was up three times is the morning your routine matters most.

Fathers are not an afterthought

We do not adapt content designed for mothers. We build content designed for fathers from the ground up. Different challenges, different biology, different expectations.

The mission

There are thousands of resources for mothers — and they deserve every one of them. But fathers get a pamphlet and a pat on the back. NextRoutine is here to close that gap.

Our mission is to make fatherhood the most well-prepared-for transition in a man's life, not the least. That means giving fathers a system that starts before the baby arrives and evolves through every stage after.
