✦ Pre-Fatherhood

Thinking about becoming a dad? Start before life gets loud.

Use this checklist and 14-day challenge to prepare calmly for money, career, time off, paternity leave, health paperwork, home routines, and the support network you may need later.

Start the 14-Day Challenge → Take the Readiness Quiz

This is not about pressure.

It is about lowering future stress. A little planning now can make pregnancy, birth, and newborn life feel less like a surprise and more like something you can step into together.

Best first move
Pick one category below and handle one checklist item this week.

Seven areas to prepare — at your own pace.

You do not need to tackle all of these at once. Pick what feels most pressing and start there.

💰 Money

Get a realistic view of baby costs before pressure rises. Review income, bills, debt, savings. Estimate first-year costs. Start a baby buffer. Check insurance deductibles.

💼 Career

Map busy seasons, deadlines, travel. Identify flexibility options. Review childcare timing. Plan how to protect family time without waiting until burnout.

📅 Time Off & Leave

Check PTO, sick days, unpaid leave, parental leave policy. Ask how leave can be split. Build a coverage plan. Save key dates and paperwork deadlines.

🏥 Health Paperwork

Review insurance for pregnancy, birth, and baby care. Know how to add baby after birth. Collect emergency contacts. Create a shared note for appointments.

🏠 Home Routines

Choose one routine to own: meals, laundry, groceries, cleaning, or bills. Create restock lists. Practice a weekly reset. Decide what can be simplified or paused.

👥 Support Network

List trusted family, friends, neighbors. Decide who can help with meals, errands, childcare. Set boundaries around visitors. Discuss what support feels helpful.

The 14-Day Challenge

Pick your priorities. The plan adapts around them so you can make progress without trying to solve everything at once.

Choose your priorities:

Money

Budget snapshot, baby costs, insurance review, savings targets.

Career

Work timeline, flexibility mapping, manager conversations, handoff planning.

Leave

PTO options, parental leave policy, income impact, HR questions.

Health

Insurance basics, provider access, deductibles, adding baby to coverage.

Relationship

Partner check-ins, shared expectations, chore handoff, protected time.

Your 14-day plan:

Day 1

Choose your top preparation priorities and write down why they matter right now.

Day 2

Create one shared note for questions, decisions, dates, and next steps.

Day 3

Handle the first task from your top priority.

Day 4

Have a 15-minute conversation about what would lower future stress.

Day 5

Handle the first task from your second priority, or deepen the first one.

Day 6

Pick one home routine you can own without reminders.

Day 7

Review the week and choose what needs a follow-up.

Day 8

Turn one loose question into a real answer: policy, cost, contact, or deadline.

Day 9

Handle the second task from your top priority.

Day 10

Map who can help later and what kind of help would actually be useful.

Day 11

Handle the second task from your second priority, or refine your plan.

Day 12

Simplify one future stress point before it becomes urgent.

Day 13

Share the plan, ask what feels missing, and adjust together.

Day 14

Choose the next two-week focus and one recurring routine to keep.
